Research Projects

IIT Delhi Coursework Projects

Below are selected research projects implemented using MATLAB and SDR platforms, focusing on RIS, OTFS, channel coding, and PHY-layer optimization.

Beyond-Diagonal RIS Connectivity Architectures – Design & Analysis

Mentor: Bhishm Tripathi (DRDO)
Status: Ongoing
Developing a generalized BD-RIS with reflective, transmissive, and hybrid modes with joint precoder design, targeting up to 75% sum-rate improvement and 20% gains over conventional RIS in MU-MISO systems.

5G NR FEC Benchmark: LDPC and Turbo Coding

Simulated LDPC and Turbo codes with belief‑propagation/MAP decoding in MATLAB to benchmark BER–SNR against convolutional codes, evaluating coding gain and 5G NR reliability/latency trade‑offs. Mentor: Prof. Harshan Jagadeesh (IIT Delhi). (Jul 2025 – Present)

6G OTFS Base Station–User Equipment Communication

Implemented and analyzed OTFS modulation with MIMO to enhance spectral efficiency and reliability in high‑mobility channels, demonstrating BER improvements and Doppler robustness via MMSE and ZF equalization. Mentor: Prof. Nethravathi KA (RVCE). (Apr 2025 – Present)


5G Baseband Pulse Shaping and ISI Reduction

Designed and simulated Raised Cosine pulse shaping filters in MATLAB to mitigate ISI, visualize eye diagrams, and optimize baseband waveform for 5G QAM symbol transmission. Mentor: Prof. Gourab Ghatak (IIT Delhi). (Jul 2025 – Aug 2025)

Adaptive Modulation for Next‑Gen Wireless Link

Implemented real‑time adaptive modulation on SDR, dynamically selecting BPSK, QPSK, and 16‑QAM based on SNR to optimize data rate and minimize BER, enhancing spectral efficiency. Mentor: Prof. Gourab Ghatak (IIT Delhi). (Jan 2025 – Apr 2025)
